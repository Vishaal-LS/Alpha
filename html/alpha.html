1. Explain Spectrochemical series.

i. Stronger ligands cause greater splitting of d-orbitals, resulting in a larger value of ΔO (crystal field splitting parameter).
ii. Weaker ligands cause smaller splitting of d-orbitals, leading to a smaller value of ΔO.
iii. The spectrochemical series arranges ligands like iodine, bromine, carbon, etc., in ascending order of their power to split d-orbitals into t2g and eg sets.
iv. The spectrochemical series order is I > Br > Cl > SCN > F > OH > C2O4 > H2O > NH3 > CN > NO2 > CO. This series helps predict the colors and properties of transition metal complexes.

2. Define Ionisation potential and explain its variation in a period and group of the periodic table.

* Ionization potential is the energy required to remove an electron from an atom or ion.
* In a period, ionization potential increases left to right due to effective nuclear charge and atomic radius.
* In a group, ionization potential decreases top to bottom due to atomic size and shielding effect.
* It reflects electron loss tendency, influenced by atomic structure, repulsion, and distance from the nucleus.

3. Explain about HSAB principle.

Hard Acids:

  • Low polarizability
  • High positive charge
  • Small size
  • Not easily oxidized

Hard Bases:

  • Low polarizability
  • Spread donor orbital
  • High electronegativity
  • Not easily oxidized

Soft Acids:

  • High polarizability
  • Low positive charge
  • Large size
  • Not easily oxidized

Soft Bases:

  • High polarizability
  • Diffuse donor orbital
  • Low electronegativity
  • Easily oxidized

Crystal Field Theory

Salient Features of Crystal Field Theory (CFT)

  1. A complex consists of a central metal ion surrounded by ligands.
  2. The metal-ligand interaction is purely electrostatic.
  3. Orbital overlap is not considered in the interaction.
  4. Ligands can be negatively charged ions or neutral molecules that approach the metal ion with negative poles closest to it.
  5. Repulsive forces between the metal ion and ligand electrons cause the splitting of d-orbitals, known as crystal field splitting.
  6. The number and arrangement of ligands determine the crystal field.
  7. Different crystal fields affect the relative energies of the five d-orbitals.

Crystal Field Splitting in Octahedral Complexes

  1. In an octahedral complex, the six ligands surround the central metal ion in an octahedral arrangement.
  2. The dx^2-y^2 and dz^2 orbitals align along the x, y, and z axes and directly face the ligands, resulting in increased repulsion.
  3. The dxy, dyz, and dzx orbitals, on the other hand, point in between the x, y, and z axes and experience less repulsion.
  4. As a result, the d-orbitals split into two sets: a more stable (lower energy) set consisting of dxy, dyz, and dzx, and a less stable (higher energy) set consisting of dx^2-y^2 and dz^2.
  5. The energy difference between the two sets of orbitals is known as crystal field stabilization energy (CFSE), represented by the symbol Δo, indicating an octahedral ligand arrangement.
